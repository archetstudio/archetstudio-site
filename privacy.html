Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 1, 2026

1. Introduction

Archet Studio LLC ("we," "our," or "us") is a California-based digital production house. Our mobile applications are currently in active development and testing and have not yet been publicly released. This Privacy Policy outlines the data practices built into our applications as well as our current data collection practices.

We are committed to a minimalist approach to data collection and have established this policy in advance of our public launches to ensure full compliance with Apple App Store, Google Play, and applicable privacy regulations from day one.

2. Scope

This policy applies to the Archet Studio LLC website and all current and future mobile applications developed and published by Archet Studio LLC, including any applications distributed through the Apple App Store and Google Play Store.

3. Current Data Collection

As our applications are currently in development and have not yet been released to the public, the only data we presently collect is through direct email inquiries sent to inquiry@archetstudio.com. Information provided in these communications is retained solely to respond to your inquiry and is not shared with any third party.

4. Application Data Practices

Our applications adhere to the Privacy Manifest and Data Minimization principles outlined in this document. The following describes the data practices built into our applications:

4.1 Microphone Audio (Transient)

Certain applications may access the device microphone for real-time audio analysis. This includes features such as pitch detection (tuner) and optional hands-free control via double-clap detection (metronome). All audio is processed entirely in-memory on the user's device using pure signal processing. It is never recorded, stored, or transmitted. No speech recognition is performed.

4.2 User Preferences (Local Only)

Our applications store user preferences (such as color theme and premium unlock status) locally on the device using AsyncStorage. This data never leaves the device and is not transmitted to any server.

4.3 Third-Party SDK Data

Our applications integrate the Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob) to serve advertisements. Banner ads are served in a non-personalized capacity. Rewarded video ads are served with personalized targeting when the user has granted consent. This SDK collects:

  • Advertising identifiers — IDFA (iOS) and AAID (Android), used to serve personalized rewarded ads and measure ad conversions
  • IP address
  • App interaction data (ad views, taps, rewarded ad completions)
  • Crash diagnostics

On iOS 14.5 and later, our applications request permission via the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework before accessing the IDFA. If permission is denied, all ads (including rewarded) are served in a non-personalized capacity. On Android, the AAID is available by default unless the user opts out via device settings.

This data is collected and processed by Google in accordance with the Google Privacy Policy. We do not have direct access to the personal data Google collects through its SDK.

4.4 Rewarded Ad Value Exchange

Our applications offer a 24-hour premium content unlock in exchange for watching a rewarded video advertisement. When a user completes a rewarded ad, we process the ad completion event to grant temporary access to premium features (e.g., additional sound packs).

  • The ad completion timestamp is stored locally on the device via AsyncStorage
  • Access expires automatically after 24 hours
  • No payment information, account credentials, or personal data is collected during this exchange
  • The AdMob SDK records the rewarded ad conversion event for ad network reporting

4.5 Geolocation Data

Our applications do not collect Precise Geolocation Data. In the event that any future feature requires temporary location access, it will be accompanied by a clear, obvious on-screen indicator as required by 2026 consumer protection regulations.

5. Data Storage & Security

Archet Studio LLC does not operate its own backend servers or cloud storage. User preferences and app state are stored locally on the device. Ad data is transmitted to Google's servers by the AdMob SDK.

  • Local storage: Preferences are stored via AsyncStorage (SQLite on Android, NSUserDefaults on iOS). This data is non-sensitive and is not encrypted at rest.
  • Network security: All network connections (AdMob) are enforced over HTTPS/TLS.
  • Data retention: Local data persists until the user uninstalls the application, at which point all local data is permanently deleted. Data collected by the AdMob SDK is retained according to Google's data retention policies.

7. Advertising & Tracking

Our applications display advertisements through Google AdMob. Banner ads are served in a non-personalized capacity. Rewarded video ads are served with personalized targeting when the user has granted consent. To enable personalized rewarded advertising, our applications collect the following advertising identifiers:

  • IDFA (Identifier for Advertisers) on iOS
  • AAID (Android Advertising ID) on Android

These identifiers are used by the AdMob SDK to serve relevant rewarded advertisements and to track rewarded video ad conversions that unlock premium content (see Section 4.4).

App Tracking Transparency (iOS)

On iOS 14.5 and later, our applications present the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt to request permission before accessing the IDFA. Users may choose to allow or deny tracking. If denied, advertisements are served in a non-personalized capacity and the IDFA is not accessed.

Ad Supply Chain Transparency

Our ad supply chain is authorized via an app-ads.txt file hosted at archetstudio.com/app-ads.txt, identifying Archet Studio LLC as a DIRECT publisher (ID: pub-8090048526672531). Google's TAG (Trustworthy Accountability Group) certification authority ID f08c47fec0942fa0 is included to provide users and advertisers with a verified, transparent ad supply chain.

To opt out of personalized ads:

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking > disable for our app
  • Android: Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization

8. Privacy Manifest & Required Reason APIs

Archet Studio LLC maintains full compliance with Apple's Privacy Manifest policy. Our applications request user consent for tracking via the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework to enable personalized advertising through AdMob.

  • NSPrivacyTracking: Set to true. Our applications request tracking permission via ATT to access the IDFA for personalized ad serving. Tracking only occurs if the user grants permission.
  • NSPrivacyTrackingDomains: Includes Google advertising domains used by the AdMob SDK for personalized ad delivery.
  • Required Reason APIs: Our applications use UserDefaults (via AsyncStorage) strictly for storing user preferences such as color theme and premium unlock timestamps. File Timestamp APIs, if accessed, are used solely for cache management of bundled assets. These APIs are never used for device fingerprinting or cross-app identification.

All current and future application releases adhere to these Privacy Manifest and Data Minimization principles.

9. Device Permissions

Our applications may request the following device permissions:

iOS

  • Microphone: Required for real-time audio analysis features such as pitch detection (tuner) and optional hands-free control via double-clap detection (metronome).
  • Background Audio: Allows continued audio playback when the app is in the background.

Android

  • Record Audio: Required for real-time audio analysis features such as pitch detection (tuner) and optional double-clap detection (metronome).
  • Modify Audio Settings: Required for audio routing during simultaneous playback and recording.
  • Foreground Service: Keeps audio features active while the app is in use. On Android 14+, sub-types FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK and FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MICROPHONE are declared to specify the service purpose.
  • Internet: Required by the AdMob SDK for ad delivery.

10. Your Rights

Our applications have not yet been publicly released. The only data we currently hold is information you voluntarily provide via email correspondence to inquiry@archetstudio.com.

All locally stored preferences are automatically deleted when the user uninstalls the application. For data collected by the AdMob SDK, users may refer to Google's privacy tools to manage their data.

California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: You may request the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to Delete: You may request deletion of personal information we hold about you. To submit a data deletion request, email inquiry@archetstudio.com with the subject line "Data Deletion Request."
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not sell personal information in the traditional sense. However, the use of advertising identifiers (IDFA/AAID) by the AdMob SDK for personalized advertising may constitute "sharing" under the CPRA. You may opt out at any time by adjusting your device settings (see Section 7) or by denying the ATT prompt on iOS.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your California privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at inquiry@archetstudio.com. We will respond within 45 days as required by California law.

11. Age Assurance and Parental Consent

Archet Studio LLC maintains compliance with the California Digital Age Assurance Act (AB 1043) and Texas SB 2420.

  • Platform Age Signals: Our applications utilize the Apple Declared Age Range API and Google Play Age Signals to respect user age brackets (Under 13, 13–15, 16–17, 18+) and adjust data practices accordingly.
  • Parental Consent: For users identified as minors by platform-level signals, our applications honor system-level "Significant Change" consent requests. No additional data processing occurs for minor users without verifiable parental or guardian consent as relayed by the platform.
  • Parental Revocation: Our applications honor parental revocation signals transmitted through Apple and Google platform APIs. When a parent or guardian revokes consent, any associated data processing ceases immediately.
  • Under 13: Our applications will not be directed at children under 13. We will not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that data from a child under 13 has been inadvertently collected, we will take steps to delete it promptly.
